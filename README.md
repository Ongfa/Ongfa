I build systems that are simple, reliable, and easy to reason about.

Most of my work sits at the intersection of frontend engineering and backend systems — designing clean UI architectures, building APIs, and recently exploring how to make LLM-based systems more predictable and structured.

I care about how systems behave in the real world:
- how they fail  
- how they recover  
- and how to make those paths clearer and more reliable  

Over the years, I’ve worked across different parts of the stack — building user-facing applications, internal tools, and backend workflows.

Lately, I’ve been exploring how to bring structure to AI systems — enforcing contracts, validating outputs, and designing backend layers that can handle non-deterministic behavior.

I like clean architecture, clear data flow, and code that still makes sense months later.

Outside of work, I enjoy building things that feel meaningful — sometimes useful, sometimes just interesting enough to explore.
